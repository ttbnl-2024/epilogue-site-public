{% extends "solution.html" %} {% load static %} {% block puzzle-author %} The Dan Miller, images by Kennedy Miller {% endblock %} {% block solution-body-md %} We are presented with two categories of information: 28 enumerations as well as 28 images (each with their own enumerations). There are a few pieces of information that lead us to the Youtube channel [Pitch Meeting](https://www.youtube.com/@PitchMeetings). This is a series of hundreds of clips, where each portrays a potential "pitch meeting" from a script writer to a movie producer about a famous movie or TV series. - The puzzle's title "Movie Effects" - The flavor text - "videos about movies are TIGHT!" (calling things "tight" is one of the catchphrases of this series) - Image 28 which has someone typing "Super easy... barely an inconvenience" into a Youtube comments section (another of the catchphrases of the series) Each of the initial enumerations is for a specific movie. We then can find the Pitch Meeting episode for that movie. In each Pitch Meeting episode, something (somewhat-) related to the movie is referred to as "tight". Each of the 28 "tight" things from the 28 movies is drawn as one of the 28 images below. For example, the enumeration (5 3) references the movie Space Jam. In the Pitch Meeting for Space Jam, `GIANT FART BALLOONS` is referred to as "tight". This is image 10 with the enumeration (5 4 8). While most (but probably not all) of the images are not easily solvable without finding the source material, the images are listed in alphabetical order of the "tight" thing. Once we have identified each of the 28 movies and the 28 images, we can notice that each image has a defined number of things in it. Following our fart balloons example, there are 5 balloons in the image. We can then index that number into the image cluephrase (`GIANT FART BALLOONS`), in this case yielding a `T`. As clued by the flavortext ("movies when they first came out"), we sort by the movie release date, yielding the cluephrase `GIRL SOFTLY TO STOP TO WASTE YOUTH`. This is a cryptic clue that fits the enumeration at the bottom of the puzzle, which indicates the wordplay and definition split. Solving yields the final answer, **`MISSPEND`** (`MISS + P + END` = "to waste youth"). {% endblock %} {% block author-notes-md %} Wow wow wow wow wow wow... wow.
