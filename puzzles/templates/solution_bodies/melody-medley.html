It is actually a five-puzzle minipuzzle set, with boundaries defined by the boxed letters. The symbols on the score are colored one of four ways: - Sections relevant to the puzzle are colored **black**, and are generally the tune to some major classical work (though the mashup tweaks key, tempo, and time signature to match the atmosphere of the mashup at the time, the contour and general rhythm should still be recognizable). - Accompaniment and other parts irrelevant to the puzzle are notated in **grey**. (They're really just there to make the whole thing sound nice, though they will generally be inspired by the accompaniment of a nearby work.) - Parts of the medley that contain hints are colored in **blue**. - There are twelve **red** symbols, whose role will be explained later in this solution. As an identification aid, and to help determine where one melody transitions to another, the initials for the composer of each melody are provided above the first note.[^1] [^1]: Initials may be confusing in places; for instance, LB (Luigi Boccherini) should not be confused with LvB (Ludwig van Beethoven), and JS (Jean Sibelius) should not be confused with the several people named Johann Strauss (Johann Strauss II is labeled JS2). Dmitri Shostakovich has been labelled DSch ([his famous musical cryptogram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DSCH_motif)) to avoid confusion with Domenico Scarlatti. Each minipuzzle, labeled as sections [A] through [E], takes place in an entirely musical setting, and will resolve to a single melody, also from classical music. Solutions to these are presented in the appendix.

The answer is **`GLASS FLUTE`**. {% endblock %} {% block author-notes-md %} There's not a lot of classical (or other art music, to be complete) in the music puzzle scene. There's probably reasons for that—classical is by far the hardest genre to research, since Musipedia (suggested by the last hunt puzzle to use a classical work, however token) has very low accuracy and I can't seem to find any other search engines for these (not to mention the lack of lyrics). I tried my best to pick either pieces in the public consciousness or pieces in the mashups, or otherwise cant towards easy discovery methods (there are only so many pieces with place names); this also meant I tended to select for melodies near the beginning of a piece for easy verification. But hey, being unable to use a search engine certainly didn't stop the community from pooling together an answer key for Grant's mashups. In the end, I think the range of puzzles here covers the field and the set of famous composers well enough[^6]—here's to more. ![]({% static 'solution_resources/melody-medley/signature.png' %}) You might notice that the answer to this puzzle is an answer in the Sedona round—Hygieia is one of Apollo's grandchildren, after all.
